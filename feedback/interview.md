# User Feedback

## How collected

In-person conversations and Facebook Messenger with fellow university students (8 people)

**When:** June 2026

---

## Raw feedback

> "Flashcards are cool but I want to mark ones I already know so I don't keep reviewing them."

> "The summary is too long sometimes. I just need the main points, not a page-long explanation."

> "Processing a 50-page PDF took like 2 minutes. I almost gave up waiting."

> "I like the chat feature — I asked it what was on page 12 and it actually answered."

> "Study rooms are useful for group study but hard to find. I didn't notice the nav link at first."

> "The dark mode is nice. Way easier on the eyes during late-night study sessions."

> "Myanmar translation is okay but some technical terms come out weird."

---

## Themes

**Flashcard UX is strong but needs more control** — everyone liked the flip-to-reveal style, but multiple users asked for marking cards as "known" or filtering by confidence level.

**Processing speed is a dealbreaker for long PDFs** — students have 30-60 page lecture slides and expect results in under 30 seconds. 2+ minutes causes drop-off.

**Output length varies too much** — some summaries are great, others feel like they just reworded the original PDF instead of condensing it. Consistency matters for study sessions.

---

## Top 3 things to fix

- [ ] Add "I know this" / "Review again" toggle on flashcards with filter options
- [ ] Improve processing speed for large PDFs (optimize chunking or show better progress)
- [ ] Tighten summary generation prompts to produce shorter, more focused output
